🏋️ POMONA RED DEVILS
Weight Room Code of Conduct & Standards
THIS ROOM IS SACRED GROUND
The weight room is where Red Devils are built. Every session is an opportunity to become stronger, tougher, and more disciplined than you were yesterday. The standards below are not suggestions — they are the price of entry. Follow them, and this room will make you great.
Rules & Standards
01
ZERO PROFANITY
No cursing. Period. The weight room demands intensity — channel it into your reps, not your mouth. How you speak reflects who you are. Red Devils carry themselves with respect.
02
LIFT WITH PURPOSE
Every rep has a reason. You are not here to just move weight — you are here to build a football player. Know your sets, know your cues, and attack each rep like it matters. Because it does.
03
FORM BEFORE LOAD
Lift heavy with good form, or reduce the weight. A sloppy rep at max weight is worthless — and dangerous. If a coach corrects your form, the weight comes down. No arguments, no excuses.
04
FULL FOCUS — NO PHONES
Phones stay in your bag or locker. The weight room is a distraction-free zone. Your Instagram can wait sixty minutes. Your development cannot. Be present — your teammates are counting on you.
05
BE ON TIME OR STAY HOME
If you arrive after the session begins, you do not enter. Late entry disrupts the group's flow and disrespects the players who showed up when they were supposed to. Punctuality is discipline.
06
RE-RACK EVERY WEIGHT
You put it up, you put it back. Every plate, every dumbbell, every collar — back where it belongs before you leave your station. The next group respects you because you respected them.
07
STICK TO THE PROGRAM
No lifts outside the designed program will be performed without explicit permission from Coach. The program exists for a reason. You do not freelance in this room. If you want to add something, ask first — the answer may surprise you.
08
COACHES HAVE FINAL SAY
When a coach speaks, you listen. Corrections are not criticism — they are coaching. The staff is here to develop you, not to challenge you. Respect the process and trust the people running it.
09
PROPER FOOTWEAR REQUIRED
Athletic shoes must be worn at all times. No sandals, slides, crocs, or open-toed footwear of any kind. Proper shoes protect your feet and keep you stable under load. Show up dressed to train or you do not train.
10
SHIRTS ON — ALWAYS
When women are present in the weight room, shirts must be worn. No exceptions. This is a matter of respect and professionalism. We train in a shared space and conduct ourselves accordingly. A Red Devil respects every person in the room.
11
NO GOOFING AROUND
No punching, pushing, shoving, or horseplay of any kind. The weight room is filled with heavy bars, loaded plates, and moving bodies — one moment of stupidity can seriously injure you or a teammate. This is a place of work. Act accordingly or leave.
12
MOVE WITH PURPOSE
When transitioning between stations, getting equipment, or moving anywhere in the weight room — jog. Walk fast at minimum. Dragging your feet wastes everyone's time and kills the energy of the room. Red Devils move like they have somewhere to be.
Rack Assignment
GROUP STRUCTURE PER RACK
3–4
Players Per Rack
1
Spotter Required
0
Unassigned Rack Time
Every player is assigned to a rack group of 3–4 athletes. You stay with your group for the full session. While one player lifts, the others are spotting, watching form, and encouraging — not standing around. Your group is your accountability unit. If one person is slacking, the whole group owns it. Take care of each other under the bar.
Consequences
🏃
Run Conditioning
Breaking any standard earns conditioning work in place of lifting. You will run — and your rack group will remember it.
🚫
Loss of Rack Time
Form breakdown or poor effort means you move to bodyweight work. The rack is a privilege earned by those who use it right.
THE RED DEVIL STANDARD
"I understand that the weight room is a place of work, discipline, and brotherhood. I will show up on time, lift with purpose, respect my teammates and coaches, and hold myself to the highest standard — because I am a Pomona Red Devil."
